About

Yiannis Vailakis joined the Department of Economics at the University of Glasgow in May 2013. Prior to this, he held academic appointments at the University of Exeter, serving as Lecturer and later Associate Professor between 2006 and 2013, and at the University of Sorbonne as a Marie Curie Fellow from 2004 to 2006. He received his PhD in Economics from the Université Catholique de Louvain in 2004.

Research

Yiannis’ research spans general equilibrium theory, macroeconomics, and optimisation, with a focus on foundational questions in economic theory. His work in general equilibrium and macroeconomics examines environments featuring asymmetric information, transaction costs, endogenous borrowing constraints, limited commitment, and sovereign debt. In optimisation, his research centres on dynamic programming and fixed-point theory, contributing to the analytical tools used to study dynamic economic environments.
